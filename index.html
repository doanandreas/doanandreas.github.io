#1 | Hot Cocoa

  • Time: 5 Minutes
  • Serves: 1 Serving
Ingredients Quantity
Granulated sugar 2 tbsp.
Cocoa powder 1 tbsp.
Milk 2 tbsp. + 1 cup, divided
Salt A pinch

Step 1: In mug, stir together sugar, cocoa powder, and 2 tablespoons of the milk until combined and there are no chunks of cocoa powder.

Step 2: Add in remaining 1 cup milk and stir.

Step 3: Microwave 1 minute. Stir then microwave for an additional 30 seconds or until hot. Add a pinch of salt and stir.

Source: The Cookful - Hot Cocoa

#2 | Chocolate Cheesecake

  • Time: 1 hour 20 Minutes
  • Serves: 8-10 Servings
Chocolate Cookie Crust:
Ingredients Quantity
Cream-filled chocolate
sandwich cookies, crushed		 18
Cup butter, melted 1/4
Sugar 1 Tbsp.
Salt A pinch
Cheesecake Filling:
Ingredients Quantity
Cream cheese, room temperature 3 (8 oz.) pkg.
Sugar 1 cup
Cornstarch 1 Tbsp.
Salt A pinch
Sour cream, room temperature 1/2 cup
Dark chocolate chips, melted 1 cup
Vanilla extract 1 Tsp.
Large eggs, room temperature 4

Step 1: Preheat the oven to 350ºF. Grease a springform pan and wrap the outside of the pan in foil. Set aside.

Chocolate Cookie Crust:

Step 1: In a bowl, mix crushed cookies, butter and sugar. Transfer to the pan and press evenly into the bottom using the bottom of a glass. Place the crust in the oven and bake for 10 minutes until the crust is slightly brown around the edges. Remove from oven and let it cool.

Cheesecake Filling:

Step 1: In a bowl, mix the cream cheese, sugar, cornstarch and salt until combined and creamy. Stir in sour cream, melted chocolate and vanilla then mix until combined. Add one egg at a time and stir to combine. Make sure to scrape the sides of the bowl and mix until smooth and creamy. Pour the cheesecake batter over the cooled crust.

Step 2: Transfer the springform pan to the middle of a large roasting pan. Carefully fill the roasting pan with about an inch of hot water. This will help the cheesecake remain creamy without cracks. Bake the cheesecake for 55-60 minutes.

Step 3: Remove cheesecake from oven. The cheesecake is done when the outer rim looks set but the middle jiggles when you gently shake the pan. Let the cheesecake cool completely on a cooling rack. This step is crucial for achieving the perfect cheesecake. Place in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or up to 72 hours.

Source: The Cookful - Best Chocolate Cheesecake

#3 | Tiramisu Cheesecake

  • Time: 1 hour 10 Minutes
  • Serves: 1 Cheesecake
Crust:
Ingredients Quantity
Coffee liqueur 1/2
Lady fingers 12
Filling:
Ingredients Quantity
Cream cheese, room temperature 2 (8 oz.) pkg.
Mascarpone, room temperature 8 oz.
Sugar 1/2 cup
Eggs 2
Vanilla extract 1/2 Tsp.
Coffee Liqueur Whipped Cream
Ingredients Quantity
Heavy whipping cream 1 cup
Coffee liqueur 1/4 cup
Powdered sugar 1/2 cup
Cocoa powder (Optional), on top
Crust:

Step 1: Preheat oven to 350ºF. Prepare a 9” springform pan by greasing and cutting parchment paper for the bottom. Set aside.

Step 2: On a shallow plate, pour a little of the 1/2 cup coffee liqueur and put a few ladyfingers on the plate, rotating to get each side. Place in springform pan once covered. Continue until all are used and bottom of the pan is filled. Lady fingers may have to be broken in order to fit.

Cheesecake Filling:

Step 1: In a large bowl, cream together the cream cheese and mascarpone with a blender. Add in sugar and blend well. Add eggs and vanilla extract and blend until just incorporated. Pour into springform pan over lady fingers.

Step 2: Bake for 35-40 minutes until edges are golden brown. If middle is still not set completely, turn oven off and open the door while letting the cheesecake cool slowly in the oven. Allow to cool completely.

Topping:

Step 1: While cheesecake is baking, blend together the whipping cream, 1/4 cup coffee liqueur and powdered sugar until hard peaks form. Set in refrigerator until ready to use.

Step 2: Once cheesecake has cooled, frost with whipped cream and dust with cocoa powder.

Source: The Cookful - Tiramisu Cheesecake
